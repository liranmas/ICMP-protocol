ICMP protocol

(Internet Control Message Protocol)

What is ICMP?


ICMP is one of the internet standards (protocol) in the TCP/IP Architecture, which reports an error in the datagram processing, meaning it is used in the network/internet layer. Jon Postel first created the definition of ICMP. He was an Internet Pioneer and was known as the "God" of the internet due to his contribution to different internet standards. After several revisions of the protocol, the final definition was published by Internet Engineering Taskforce in September 1981 in RFC777.

 

The ICMP serves as a diagnosis protocol for network communication issues by generating error messages and information about the connection being successful or failure when communicating with another IP address. ICMP must be enforced and implemented by all IP modules because it is an essential part of the IP definition.
